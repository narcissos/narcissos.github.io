Sonic Essay

AmandaMonasar · How a Bunch of Skeletons Inspired me to Write

[Sound Effect: Spacebar]

It all started with Undertale.

[Music: Undertale by Toby Fox]

The reason I became so well-rounded on the internet and actively try to grow as a digital writer started with Undertale in 2015. At first glance, it was just a funny game with talking skeletons named after fonts.

[Music stops]

Until it wasn’t.

[Music: Club Moka by Blue Dot Sessions]

There was a moral to be learned. A story to walk away with. One so moving, I thought about it for weeks afterwards. I couldn’t even listen to the soundtrack without shedding a tear.

And for me, that was an inspiring moment. I wanted to leave my own mark on the world through good writing. Obviously, I knew my magnum opus wasn’t going to come immediately, but it had to start somewhere, right? I decided it was time to go on a journey and find my way there.

[Music: Slow Rollout by Blue Dot Sessions]

Social media was the doorway into the digital world, but I didn’t start taking it seriously until Tumblr. I learned that digital writing is much more than just text. It also includes aesthetics, multimodality, and carefully combining them together.

I tried WordPress but it didn’t really work out the same way. I couldn’t figure out what content I should even be posting since all my interests already had blogs dedicated to them. What made me any different? I quickly got discouraged, and back to Tumblr I went.

I was even a fanfic author. I spent a lot of time learning to write for an audience and eventually racked up a decent number of reads. A large part of this included accepting criticism to better my writing, and interacting with avid readers in a professional, yet personal way.

[Music: Night Watch by Blue Dot Sessions]

During this time of self-discovery, I even collaborated with a team of artists to make a digital charity zine for an LGBT shelter in South Korea. We raised a few hundred dollars off a very limited run, and I was stunned when I realized writing had connected me across the globe to Asia.

I kept trying new things for the sake of it. Art, Photoshop, Video-Editing, I even designed a book with a follower of mine now available at Indigo. Each one of these experiences became a notch on my belt. Eventually, I realized something, both about digital authoring and about myself.

[Music stops]

It’s a process.

[Music: In the Box by Blue Dot Sessions]

Digital authoring taught me about authenticity. I’m not saying spill your life story out for total strangers… but who cares about telling universal stories that everyone can relate to? What about your own story? Whether it’s your gender, ethnicity, age, or anything else… there’s value in your experience, shared or not.

I keep learning to think more critically about my identity and who I want to be on the internet. To present my own authentic self and tap into the everchanging cyberspace around us.

[Music: Speaker Joy by Blue Dot Sessions]

Lately, I’ve been interested in visual novels—text-based video games featuring art, music, and interactivity. Readers have stakes as they alter the story with their choices. Open-sourced programs like Twine and game engines like Unity have given me these affordances.

Of course, learning the required languages of JavaScript and Python is easier said than done, but with a little (or a lot of) hard work… it’s within reach.

And I’m sure, either in a few weeks or months or years, I’ll find something else that interests me, and keep learning and growing as a digital author. Because remember. It’s all a process.

My name is Amanda, and this has been my digital authoring practices.

[Music stops]
